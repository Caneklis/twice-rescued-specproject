This statuette probably depicting a captive slave was acquired by William von Bode in London in 1895. Published by him in the same year, the statue firmly entered the body of the works of Bertoldo di Giovanni (c. 1440-1491), continuing to be mentioned in the literature even after its disappearance in 1945, always as an autograph work. The young man is seen here in an awkward position with his legs in shackles; he cannot be clearly identified, and the exact meaning of the object that he holds in his hand is also unclear. There is an opinion that the statue could be part of a pair.

The metal composition was determined. The surface was cleaned and consolidated.
